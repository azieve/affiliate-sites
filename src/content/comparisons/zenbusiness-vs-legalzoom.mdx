## Pricing Comparison

Both ZenBusiness and LegalZoom advertise $0 LLC formation, but the real cost of forming and maintaining your LLC depends on which plan you choose and what add-ons you need. Let's break down the actual numbers.

### ZenBusiness Pricing

ZenBusiness offers three plans:

- **Starter ($0 + state fee):** Includes articles of organization filing and an operating agreement template. This is a genuinely free plan, though it uses standard filing speed (2-3 weeks) and does not include registered agent service.
- **Pro ($199/year + state fee):** Adds Worry-Free Compliance monitoring, EIN filing, expedited filing speed, and a banking resolution. This is the plan most business owners end up choosing because compliance tracking alone can save you from costly missed deadlines.
- **Premium ($349/year + state fee):** Includes everything in Pro plus a business website, domain name, and business email address. Worth it if you don't already have a web presence.

### LegalZoom Pricing

LegalZoom also offers three tiers:

- **Basic ($0 + state fee):** Includes articles of organization filing, a name availability search, and a digital welcome packet. This plan is notably bare-bones - it doesn't include an operating agreement, which is a document most LLCs need.
- **Pro ($249 + state fee):** Adds EIN filing, an operating agreement, a banking resolution, and express shipping of documents. Note that this is a one-time fee, not a recurring annual charge.
- **Premium ($399 + state fee):** Includes everything in Pro plus a business plan template, trademark search, unlimited 30-minute attorney consultations, and an annual compliance calendar.

### The Real Cost Comparison

Where the pricing difference becomes most apparent is with registered agent service. Every LLC needs a registered agent, and neither service includes one for free:

- **ZenBusiness registered agent:** $199/year
- **LegalZoom registered agent:** $299/year

That $100 annual difference adds up. Over five years, you'd pay $500 more with LegalZoom just for registered agent service.

When you compare the mid-tier plans (which is what most people end up needing), ZenBusiness Pro at $199/year gives you compliance monitoring, EIN filing, and expedited processing. LegalZoom Pro at $249 includes similar features but costs $50 more and does not include ongoing compliance tracking.

**Pricing winner: ZenBusiness.** It's consistently more affordable, and the recurring cost difference in registered agent service compounds over time.

## Features Comparison

Beyond pricing, the features included at each tier tell an important story about what you're actually getting for your money.

### Formation Essentials

Both services handle the basics of LLC formation across all 50 states. They'll prepare and file your articles of organization with the state, which is the core document that creates your LLC.

Where they diverge is in what else comes with the base plan:

- **ZenBusiness Starter** includes an operating agreement template at $0. This is a significant advantage since an operating agreement is essential for establishing how your LLC operates, and many banks require one to open a business account.
- **LegalZoom Basic** does not include an operating agreement. You'll need to upgrade to their $249 Pro plan to get one, or draft one yourself.

### Compliance and Ongoing Support

Staying compliant after formation is just as important as forming the LLC itself. Missing an annual report deadline or failing to update your state records can result in penalties or even losing your LLC's good standing.

- **ZenBusiness** includes Worry-Free Compliance in their Pro plan ($199/year), which monitors your filing deadlines and sends alerts so you don't miss anything. They also handle BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) report filing.
- **LegalZoom** offers compliance features only in their Premium plan ($399). Their Basic and Pro plans don't include compliance calendar tools. BOI report filing is available as a paid add-on rather than an included feature.

### Legal Services

This is where LegalZoom genuinely stands out. Their Premium plan ($399) includes unlimited 30-minute attorney consultations, a trademark search, and a business plan template. If you anticipate needing legal guidance beyond formation — contract reviews, trademark questions, or ongoing legal advice — LegalZoom's legal service ecosystem is deeper than anything ZenBusiness offers.

ZenBusiness does not offer attorney consultations or direct legal services. If you need legal advice, you'll need to find an attorney separately.

## Customer Support & Experience

### Support Channels

Both ZenBusiness and LegalZoom offer phone, email, and live chat support. Neither service is available 24/7 — phone support at both companies is limited to business hours.

In practice, ZenBusiness tends to deliver a more streamlined support experience. Their team is focused exclusively on business formation and compliance, so representatives are generally well-versed in the common questions new business owners have.

LegalZoom's support covers a broader range of legal services, which means you might get routed through more menus before reaching someone who can help with your specific LLC question. That said, their support staff has access to legal resources that ZenBusiness's team does not, which can be valuable for more complex situations.

### User Experience

ZenBusiness has the edge when it comes to the formation process itself. Their interface is clean and modern, with a step-by-step flow that walks you through each decision. The dashboard after formation makes it easy to see what's been filed, what's pending, and what deadlines are coming up.

LegalZoom's interface has improved over the years but still feels more complex. There are more screens, more options, and more upsells during checkout. The sheer breadth of LegalZoom's service catalog means the experience is less focused than ZenBusiness's formation-centric approach.

### Upselling

Both services engage in upselling during the checkout process, but it's a common complaint for both. ZenBusiness will suggest add-ons like registered agent service, website packages, and banking services. LegalZoom tends to be more aggressive, presenting multiple screens of add-ons including legal plans, trademark filings, and compliance packages.

Neither experience is terrible, but be prepared to click "no thanks" several times with either service.

**Support winner: ZenBusiness**, for a more focused and less cluttered experience. LegalZoom has the advantage if you need access to legal expertise.

## Formation Speed

How quickly your LLC gets filed depends on two factors: how fast the service processes your order and how long your state takes to process the filing.

### ZenBusiness Processing Times

- **Standard (Starter plan):** 2-3 weeks
- **Expedited (Pro plan):** 4-6 business days
- **Rush (Premium plan):** Fastest available processing

ZenBusiness's standard processing time is the main drawback of their free Starter plan. If you need your LLC formed quickly, you'll need to upgrade to Pro or Premium, or pay for expedited processing as an add-on.

### LegalZoom Processing Times

- **Standard:** 7-10 business days
- **Expedited:** 5-7 business days

LegalZoom's standard processing is actually faster than ZenBusiness's standard tier. If you're on the free plan with both services, LegalZoom will typically get your paperwork to the state sooner.

However, when you compare expedited options, ZenBusiness's Pro plan (4-6 days) is competitive with LegalZoom's expedited tier (5-7 days), and the ZenBusiness Pro plan includes other valuable features like compliance monitoring and EIN filing.

**Speed winner: LegalZoom** at the free tier. At paid tiers, it's roughly a tie.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ZenBusiness actually better than LegalZoom?

For most people forming a straightforward LLC, yes. ZenBusiness offers lower prices, more features per plan tier, and higher customer satisfaction ratings. LegalZoom is better if you need access to attorneys or additional legal services beyond formation.

### Are ZenBusiness and LegalZoom both legitimate?

Absolutely. Both are well-established companies that have helped hundreds of thousands of people form LLCs. ZenBusiness has been operating since 2015 with a 4.8-star Trustpilot rating. LegalZoom has been in business since 2001 and is publicly traded. Either service will handle your LLC formation correctly.

### How much does it really cost to form an LLC with each service?

Both offer $0 base formation, but you'll need to add state filing fees (which vary by state) and likely a registered agent. A realistic budget with ZenBusiness Pro is $199/year plus your state fee. With LegalZoom Pro, expect $249 plus state fee, plus $299/year if you need registered agent service. Over the first year, ZenBusiness is typically $150-$200 cheaper for comparable features.

### Can I switch from LegalZoom to ZenBusiness (or vice versa)?

Yes. Your LLC is filed with the state, not tied to a formation service. You can switch your registered agent to a different provider at any time by filing a change of agent form with your state. Any compliance or monitoring services are separate subscriptions you can cancel and replace.

### Do I need the paid plans, or is the free tier enough?

The free tier at either service will get your LLC formed, but most business owners end up needing at least an EIN (required for business bank accounts, hiring employees, and most tax situations) and compliance tracking. The free plans at both ZenBusiness and LegalZoom don't include these, so upgrading to a mid-tier plan is usually worthwhile.

### Which service is faster at forming an LLC?

LegalZoom's standard processing (7-10 business days) is faster than ZenBusiness's standard speed (2-3 weeks). However, ZenBusiness's expedited processing (4-6 business days with the Pro plan) is comparable to LegalZoom's expedited option (5-7 business days). If speed is critical and you don't want to pay for a higher plan, LegalZoom has the edge.

### Does either service offer a money-back guarantee?

Both ZenBusiness and LegalZoom offer money-back guarantees if you're not satisfied with their service. Terms and conditions vary, so review the specific guarantee details on each company's website before purchasing.

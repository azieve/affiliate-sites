## What Is LLC Attorney?

LLC Attorney is an attorney-backed LLC formation service founded in 2020. Unlike traditional online formation companies that rely entirely on automated systems to generate and file your documents, LLC Attorney involves licensed attorneys in the document preparation process.

Their core value proposition is straightforward: you get the convenience of an online formation service combined with the legal oversight that comes from having actual lawyers review your paperwork. This positions them somewhere between a fully automated service like [ZenBusiness](/reviews/zenbusiness/) and hiring a local business attorney at $200-500 per hour.

The company covers all 50 states and offers a single plan structure, which keeps the decision-making simple. There are no tiered packages to compare or confusing upgrade paths to navigate.

## What's Included

Let me walk through each component of the LLC Attorney Standard plan.

### Articles of Organization Filing

LLC Attorney prepares and files your Articles of Organization (or Certificate of Formation, depending on your state) with your state's Secretary of State. This is the foundational document that legally creates your LLC. The difference here is that an attorney reviews the filing before submission, which can catch errors or omissions that automated systems might miss.

### Attorney-Drafted Operating Agreement

This is the headline feature and the primary reason to consider LLC Attorney over cheaper alternatives.

An [operating agreement](/learn/llc-operating-agreement/) defines how your LLC is owned, managed, and operated. It covers profit distribution, voting rights, member responsibilities, and what happens if a member leaves or the business dissolves.

Most formation services provide a generic template that you fill in with basic details. LLC Attorney provides an operating agreement that has been drafted with attorney oversight, which means it's more likely to address common legal pitfalls and comply with your state's specific LLC statutes.

This distinction matters most for multi-member LLCs, where the operating agreement governs the relationship between business partners. A poorly drafted operating agreement can lead to expensive disputes down the road. For single-member LLCs, the stakes are lower, but having a well-drafted document still supports your liability protection.

### EIN Filing

LLC Attorney includes EIN (Employer Identification Number) filing in the base package. Your EIN is essentially a Social Security number for your business -- you need it to open a business bank account, hire employees, and file taxes.

This is a meaningful inclusion. [Northwest Registered Agent](/reviews/northwest-registered-agent/) charges extra for EIN filing, and at ZenBusiness, you need the Pro plan to get it. You can always file for an EIN yourself for free through the IRS website, but having it handled as part of the formation process saves a step.

### Compliance Monitoring

LLC Attorney includes ongoing compliance monitoring that tracks important deadlines for your business -- annual report filings, state fee due dates, and other regulatory requirements. Missing these deadlines can result in late fees, penalties, or even administrative dissolution of your LLC.

### Registered Agent Service (1 Year)

Every LLC must designate a registered agent to receive legal documents and government notices. LLC Attorney includes one year of registered agent service in the $49 package, with renewal at $100/year after that.

The $100/year renewal rate compares favorably to the competition. LegalZoom charges $299/year, ZenBusiness charges $199/year, and Northwest charges $125/year.

### BOI Report Filing

LLC Attorney includes Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report filing. Most LLCs are required to file this report with FinCEN (the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network). Having it handled as part of the formation package removes another item from your post-formation to-do list.

### What's Not Included

LLC Attorney does not include **privacy protection** on your formation filings. This means your name and address will appear on publicly searchable state records. If privacy is a top concern, Northwest Registered Agent includes this feature in their $39 package and is worth considering.

## Attorney-Assisted vs DIY Formation

The central question with LLC Attorney is whether attorney involvement in the formation process is worth paying for. Here's how I think about it.

**When attorney oversight adds real value:**

- **Multi-member LLCs.** When two or more people co-own a business, the operating agreement becomes a contract between partners. Having an attorney-drafted agreement that addresses profit splits, dispute resolution, buyout provisions, and dissolution procedures can prevent costly legal battles later.
- **Complex ownership structures.** If your LLC involves investors, holding companies, or unusual profit-sharing arrangements, template documents may not adequately address your situation.
- **Regulated industries.** Certain industries have specific formation requirements or compliance obligations that benefit from legal review.
- **Asset protection concerns.** If you're forming an LLC specifically to protect personal assets, attorney oversight can help ensure your formation documents support that goal.

**When a DIY or automated service is probably fine:**

- **Simple single-member LLCs.** If you're a freelancer, consultant, or solopreneur forming a single-member LLC with a straightforward structure, the legal complexity is low. A template operating agreement from Northwest or ZenBusiness is likely sufficient.
- **Budget is the primary constraint.** If you're starting with minimal capital and every dollar matters, the $0 formation services from ZenBusiness or Bizee get the job done.
- **You're comfortable with legal documents.** If you have experience with business formation or are willing to research your state's LLC requirements, automated filing works well.

**Important note:** LLC Attorney's service should not be confused with hiring a dedicated business attorney for personalized legal advice. While attorneys are involved in reviewing and drafting documents, this is a standardized formation service -- not a substitute for a one-on-one consultation with a lawyer about your specific legal situation.

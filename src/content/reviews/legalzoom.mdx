LegalZoom is the name most people think of when they hear "online legal services." Founded in 2001, the company has spent over two decades building brand recognition through television ads, partnerships, and sheer market presence. But when it comes to LLC formation specifically, is LegalZoom actually worth it -- or are you paying a premium for the name?

In this LegalZoom LLC review, I'll break down what you actually get at each price tier, where LegalZoom delivers genuine value, and where cheaper competitors offer the same or better service for less money. I've used LegalZoom alongside four other major formation services, so this review is based on firsthand experience rather than marketing copy.

**The short version:** LegalZoom offers a $0 Basic formation plan, but it's extremely bare-bones. The real costs show up quickly -- registered agent service is $299/year (the most expensive among major providers), and you'll need the $249 Pro plan to get essentials like an operating agreement and EIN filing. LegalZoom is a solid choice if you want access to attorney consultations and a broad legal services ecosystem, but most people forming a simple LLC will find better value elsewhere.

## Quick Verdict

LegalZoom earns an **8.5 out of 10** in our ratings. The brand is trustworthy, the legal service add-ons are genuinely useful for some business owners, and the company has a long track record. However, the high cost of registered agent service, aggressive upselling during checkout, and the stripped-down Basic plan hold it back from competing with more focused, affordable alternatives.

If you're forming a straightforward LLC and don't need ongoing attorney access, services like [Northwest Registered Agent](/reviews/northwest-registered-agent/) or [ZenBusiness](/reviews/zenbusiness/) offer more for less. If you want the comfort of a well-known brand with access to a broader legal services platform, LegalZoom has a place.

## What Is LegalZoom?

LegalZoom is an online legal technology company that offers a wide range of services beyond LLC formation -- including trademark registration, estate planning, intellectual property, and business compliance. They were founded in 2001 by attorneys who saw an opportunity to make legal services more accessible to everyday consumers and small business owners.

That breadth is both LegalZoom's strength and its weakness for LLC formation specifically. The platform is built to serve as a one-stop legal shop, which means the LLC formation experience often feels like an entry point designed to funnel you into higher-margin services. More on that later.

LegalZoom covers all 50 states, has a 4.5-star rating on Trustpilot from over 8,000 reviews, and offers phone, email, and live chat support.

## LegalZoom LLC Pricing Plans

LegalZoom uses a three-tier pricing model. Here's what each plan costs and includes:

### Basic Plan -- $0 (+ State Filing Fee)

- Articles of Organization filing
- Name availability search
- Digital welcome packet

That's it. The Basic plan gets your LLC filed with the state, and not much else. There's no operating agreement, no EIN filing, no registered agent service, and no compliance support. It's a loss leader designed to get you into the LegalZoom ecosystem.

### Pro Plan -- $249 (+ State Filing Fee)

- Everything in Basic
- EIN filing
- Operating agreement
- Banking resolution
- Express shipping of documents

The Pro plan is where LegalZoom starts to feel like a complete formation service. The operating agreement and EIN filing are essentials that most business owners need, and it's worth noting that competitors like [Northwest Registered Agent](/reviews/northwest-registered-agent/) include an operating agreement in their $39 base package.

### Premium Plan -- $399 (+ State Filing Fee)

- Everything in Pro
- Business plan template
- Trademark search
- Unlimited attorney consultations (30 minutes each)
- Annual compliance calendar

The Premium plan is LegalZoom's flagship, and the attorney consultations are the main draw. If you anticipate needing ongoing legal guidance as you build your business, the ability to speak with an attorney in 30-minute sessions has real value. The compliance calendar is also helpful, though other services include compliance alerts at lower price points.

### The Hidden Cost: Registered Agent Service

Here's the number that matters most for your ongoing costs: LegalZoom's registered agent service is **$299/year**, and it's not included in any plan. Every LLC needs a registered agent, so this is effectively a mandatory add-on.

For comparison:

- **Northwest Registered Agent:** Free first year, then $125/year
- **ZenBusiness:** $199/year
- **Bizee:** Free first year, then renewal pricing applies

Over three years, that $299/year registered agent fee adds up to $897 with LegalZoom, compared to $289 with Northwest ($0 first year + $125 x 2). That's a $608 difference on registered agent service alone.

## LegalZoom Registered Agent Service

Since registered agent service is separate from every LegalZoom plan, it deserves its own section.

At $299/year, LegalZoom's registered agent service is the most expensive option among the major formation providers. The service itself is standard -- they receive legal documents and government correspondence on behalf of your LLC and forward them to you. You get access to an online portal where you can view received documents.

The service works fine. But there's nothing about it that justifies a price nearly three times what Northwest charges after their free first year. If you form your LLC through LegalZoom, I'd recommend using a separate, more affordable registered agent provider unless the convenience of having everything under one roof is worth the extra cost to you.

## The Formation Experience

Here's what it's actually like to form an LLC through LegalZoom.

### The Checkout Process

This is where LegalZoom lost me during testing. The formation flow itself is straightforward enough -- you enter your LLC name, select your state, provide member information, and choose your plan. But between each step, you're presented with add-on offers.

Registered agent service. Rush processing. Compliance packages. Operating agreement upgrades. Business license reports. Trademark monitoring. The offers are persistent, and some are presented in ways that make it easy to accidentally add them to your cart.

I counted over a dozen upsell screens during my checkout process. If you're comfortable clicking "No thanks" repeatedly, you'll get through it fine. But if you're a first-time business owner who isn't sure what you need, it's easy to end up spending significantly more than you planned.

### Filing and Processing Time

LegalZoom's standard processing time is **7-10 business days** before your documents are submitted to the state. This is on the slower side compared to competitors. Northwest processes filings the same day, and even ZenBusiness and Bizee are faster at their standard speeds.

If you need faster processing, LegalZoom offers expedited filing at an additional cost, which brings the timeline down to 5-7 business days. That's still slower than Northwest's same-day processing, which is included in their base price.

### After Formation

Once your LLC is approved, you receive a digital welcome packet and access to your LegalZoom dashboard. The dashboard is well-designed and easy to navigate -- this is one area where LegalZoom's investment in user experience shows. You can view your formation documents, manage your services, and access any add-ons you've purchased.

## Customer Support & Legal Services

LegalZoom offers customer support through phone, email, and live chat. My experience with support was mixed. Wait times on the phone were longer than what I experienced with Northwest -- I waited about 15 minutes during one call. The representatives were knowledgeable but occasionally seemed more focused on suggesting additional services than answering my specific question.

Where LegalZoom genuinely differentiates itself is the legal services ecosystem. The Premium plan's attorney consultations give you access to real attorneys for 30-minute sessions. If you're dealing with a contract question, a partnership dispute, or need guidance on a legal issue affecting your business, this is a meaningful benefit that formation-only services don't offer.

LegalZoom also offers ongoing legal services like trademark registration, contract review, and estate planning. If you anticipate needing multiple legal services as your business grows, having them available through a single platform has real convenience value.

## LegalZoom Pros and Cons

### What LegalZoom Does Well

**1. Brand recognition and trust.** LegalZoom is the most recognized name in online legal services. For business owners who value working with an established, well-known company, that familiarity provides peace of mind. They've been operating since 2001 and have served millions of customers.

**2. $0 Basic formation option.** If you just need articles of organization filed and nothing else, LegalZoom will do it for the cost of your state filing fee. The Basic plan is bare-bones, but it exists.

**3. Attorney consultations.** The Premium plan's unlimited 30-minute attorney consultations are a genuine differentiator. No other major formation service offers this level of legal access as part of a formation package.

**4. Broad legal services ecosystem.** Trademark registration, contract review, estate planning, compliance services -- LegalZoom offers legal services well beyond LLC formation. If you need a one-stop legal shop, they're the closest thing available.

**5. Long track record.** Over 20 years in business with millions of customers served. LegalZoom isn't going anywhere, and that stability matters when you're entrusting a company with your business formation.

### Where LegalZoom Falls Short

**1. Registered agent pricing is the highest in the industry.** At $299/year with no free introductory period, LegalZoom's registered agent service costs significantly more than every major competitor.

**2. Aggressive upselling throughout checkout.** The number of add-on offers during the formation process is excessive. First-time business owners are particularly vulnerable to adding services they don't need.

**3. The Basic plan is genuinely bare-bones.** No operating agreement, no EIN filing, no registered agent, no compliance support. The $0 price tag is misleading if you need a reasonably complete formation package.

**4. Customer support can be slow.** Phone wait times were longer than competitors, and interactions sometimes felt sales-oriented.

**5. Slower standard processing.** At 7-10 business days before state submission, LegalZoom is one of the slower services at their standard tier. Northwest files the same day at no extra charge.

**6. No privacy protection.** LegalZoom does not offer the option to use an alternate address on your public formation documents. Your personal information will appear on state filings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is LegalZoom legit?

Yes. LegalZoom has been in business since 2001, has served millions of customers, and holds a 4.5-star rating on Trustpilot with over 8,000 reviews. They're a legitimate, well-established company. The question isn't whether LegalZoom is trustworthy -- it's whether their pricing offers good value compared to alternatives.

### How much does it cost to form an LLC through LegalZoom?

LegalZoom's Basic plan is $0 plus your state filing fee. The Pro plan is $249 plus state fees, and the Premium plan is $399 plus state fees. Registered agent service is an additional $299/year and is not included in any plan. The total first-year cost for a Pro plan with registered agent service would be $548 plus your state filing fee.

### Does LegalZoom include a registered agent?

No. Registered agent service is a separate $299/year add-on at every plan level. This is the most expensive registered agent pricing among the major formation providers.

### Does LegalZoom include an operating agreement?

Not in the Basic plan. An operating agreement is included starting with the Pro plan at $249. Since an operating agreement is an essential document for virtually every LLC, this means the Basic plan is incomplete for most business owners.

### How long does LegalZoom take to form an LLC?

LegalZoom's standard processing time is 7-10 business days before documents are submitted to the state. Expedited processing (5-7 business days) is available at additional cost. After LegalZoom submits your filing, actual approval depends on your state's processing speed.

### Does LegalZoom offer a money-back guarantee?

Yes. LegalZoom offers a satisfaction guarantee on their formation services.

### Is LegalZoom worth it in 2026?

It depends on what you need. If you want attorney consultations and access to a broad legal services platform, LegalZoom's Premium plan offers unique value. For straightforward LLC formation, competitors like Northwest Registered Agent and ZenBusiness provide more features at lower prices. The $299/year registered agent fee is the biggest ongoing cost concern.

### Can I use LegalZoom for an LLC in any state?

Yes. LegalZoom offers LLC formation services in all 50 states. State filing fees vary and are charged in addition to LegalZoom's service fees.

### Does LegalZoom handle BOI reporting?

BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) report filing is available through LegalZoom as an add-on service. It is not included in any of the standard formation plans. Other providers like Northwest include BOI filing in their base package.

## Our Bottom Line

LegalZoom is a reputable company with a long track record and the broadest range of legal services among formation providers. The Premium plan's attorney consultations are a genuine differentiator that no other major competitor matches.

But for the core task of forming an LLC, LegalZoom charges premium prices without delivering premium value. The Basic plan leaves out essentials. The registered agent service is the most expensive in the industry at $299/year. The checkout process is cluttered with upsells. And the standard processing speed trails behind most competitors.

If attorney access and a broad legal platform matter to you, LegalZoom's Premium plan at $399 is worth considering. For everyone else forming a straightforward LLC, you'll get better value from [Northwest Registered Agent](/reviews/northwest-registered-agent/) ($39 with privacy protection and same-day filing) or [ZenBusiness](/reviews/zenbusiness/) ($0 Starter plan with more included features).

LegalZoom built its reputation on being the first major player in online legal services. In 2026, being first is no longer enough when newer competitors offer more for less.

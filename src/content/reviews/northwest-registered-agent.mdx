## What's Included in Northwest's Formation Package

Let me break down each component of what you actually receive.

### Articles of Organization Filing

This is the core service -- Northwest prepares and files your Articles of Organization (sometimes called a Certificate of Formation, depending on your state) with your state's Secretary of State. They handle the paperwork, formatting requirements, and submission. You provide the basic information about your LLC, and they take care of the rest.

Northwest processes filings the same day you complete your order, which means your documents reach the state as quickly as possible. Actual approval time depends on your state's processing speed, which can range from a few business days to several weeks.

### Free Registered Agent Service (1 Year)

Every LLC is legally required to have a [registered agent](/learn/what-is-a-registered-agent/) -- someone designated to receive legal documents, tax notices, and official government correspondence on behalf of your business. Northwest includes a full year of registered agent service at no additional cost.

After the first year, the service renews at $125/year. That's more expensive than some alternatives, but it's in line with industry averages and considerably less than LegalZoom's $299/year.

### Privacy Protection

This is where Northwest genuinely stands out, and it's the feature that made me a long-term customer.

When you file your LLC, your formation documents become public record. That means your name and address are searchable by anyone. Most formation services file these documents using the information you provide -- your personal home address ends up on a publicly accessible state database.

Northwest uses their own business address on your formation documents instead of yours. This keeps your personal address off public records, which is a meaningful privacy benefit. You don't need to set up a P.O. box or rent a virtual office to keep your home address private.

This privacy protection is included in the $39 formation package at no extra cost. Other services either don't offer this or charge additional fees for it.

### Operating Agreement

Northwest provides an operating agreement template as part of the formation process. An operating agreement outlines the ownership structure and operating procedures of your LLC. While not every state legally requires one, having an operating agreement is considered a best practice -- it helps establish that your LLC is a legitimate, separate entity from you personally, which supports your liability protection.

### BOI Report Filing

As of 2024, most LLCs are required to file a Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network ([FinCEN](https://www.fincen.gov/boi)). Northwest includes BOI report filing as part of their service, which saves you from navigating the FinCEN reporting system on your own.

### Compliance Alerts

Northwest sends you reminders about important deadlines -- annual report filings, registered agent renewals, and other state-specific compliance requirements. Missing these deadlines can result in late fees or even administrative dissolution of your LLC, so these alerts are genuinely useful.
